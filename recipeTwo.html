Salted Chocolate Chip Cookies



Recipe

8.5 ounces Cake Flour
8.5 ounces Bread Flour
1 1/4 teaspoon Baking Soda
1 1/2 teaspoon Baking Powder
1 teaspoon Salt
2 teaspoons Flakey Sea Salt (Reserved for Topping)
1/2 teaspoon Espresso Powder
1 1/4 sticks of Butter
10 ounces of Light Brown Sugar
8 ounces of White Sugar
2 large Brown Eggs
2 teaspoons Vanilla Extract
8 ounces Semi-Sweet Chocolate
8 ounces Milk Chocolate



Directions

  1. Start off by sifting the bread flour, cake flour, baking soda, baking powder, salt and, espresso powder into a medium sized bowl.
  2. Cut your butter into tablespoons by following the guide on the package. In the bowl of a stand mixer, add your butter, white sugar, and brown sugar. Cream these together until light and fluffy, about 5-6 minutes.
  3. Once the butter and sugar is creamed, add the eggs one at a time, then slowly add the vanilla.
  4. Add the sifted flour mixture to the wet ingredients and slowly fold until almost combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this will make your cookies tough!
  5. Roughly chop your semi-sweet and milk chocolate into suitable sized chunks.
  6. Add these into your cookie dough, folding them in to combine the rest of the flour and distribute the chunks.
  7. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let the dough rest overnight.
  8. Preheat the oven to 325 degrees fahrenheit, and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  9. Use a cookie dough scoop to scoop out the dough onto a baking sheet, placing them about 2-3 inches apart.
  10. Place the cookies into the heated oven and bake for 9 to 10 minutes. Then, carefully open the oven, rotate the baking sheet until the cookies that were in the front are now near the back of the oven. Finally, pick the baking sheet up about 1/2 inch off of the oven rack and lightly drop it. Continue to bake the cookies for another 9 to 10 minutes.
  11. Take the cookies out of the oven when they are lightly golden brown, transfer them onto a wire rack to cool, and immediately sprinkle them with the flaky salt. Cool for 10 minutes.

    12. That's it! For an extra treat, pour a tall glass of milk and add a small splash of vanilla. Dunk your cookies in your vanilla milk, and enjoy!


This recipe is inspired by the New York Time's cookies.
